Modern Medicine

In the 19th century, medicine turned from guesswork into science. Edward Jenner’s vaccine for smallpox showed that diseases could be prevented, while Louis Pasteur’s germ theory revealed the invisible world of microbes. Joseph Lister’s use of antiseptics in surgery saved countless lives and reshaped hospitals forever.

These discoveries became the roots of modern healthcare. From vaccines to antibiotics, the Industrial era’s breakthroughs made life expectancy soar. Every hospital, clinic, and research lab today still builds on the same scientific shift that began back then.
