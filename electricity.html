Electrification

The rise of electricity changed more than industriesâ€”it changed everyday life. When Edison built his first power station in 1882, it brought light to New York City and symbolized a new kind of progress. But the real breakthrough came with alternating current, developed by Nikola Tesla and promoted by George Westinghouse, which allowed electricity to travel over long distances.

Electric power fueled machines, homes, and eventually the communication networks that shape our world. It made cities safer at night, factories more productive, and homes more connected. Every light switch, motor, and circuit today is part of the same legacy that began in the Industrial Revolution.
